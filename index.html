About Me

Finding the Gap. Building the Thing. Bringing People Along.

I was fifteen when I built my first website on WordPress. Nobody asked me to. My community needed a place to find information and I just thought, why not me? It was rough. But people used it.

I didn't plan a career in marketing. I fell into it. Blogging led to Twitter, where I grew to 160k followers. That led to influencer work, then social media management, then full campaigns, then strategy. One thing kept pulling the next because I was enjoying marketing.

Somewhere between Nigeria and Canada, across banks, charities, a media company, a crypto startup, and a university alumni office, I figured out what I was actually good at: understanding what people need and building something they want to be part of.

The wins are real. A billion impressions for Heineken Nigeria ahead of the 2018 FIFA World Cup. Over 250% audience growth in two years at First City Monument Bank. A scholarship from the Government of Canada to earn my Chartered Marketer designation. And most recently, helping the University of Lethbridge grow its alumni audience by over 120% while improving event participation and fundraising.

But the projects I think about most are the ones nobody hired me to do. BUBO FPL, for people who live and breathe football. Drive Thru, a newsletter for immigrants figuring out life in Canada. No brief, no budget. Just a gap I couldn't walk past.

Same instinct, different scale. I'm still that fifteen-year-old. Finding the gap, building the thing, bringing people along.
